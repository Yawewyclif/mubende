    HISTORY OF MUBENDE

    Mubende District holds a rich and unique place in Uganda's history. Located in the Central Region, Mubende is known for its cultural and spiritual heritage, deeply connected to the ancient Bachwezi dynasty and the Buganda Kingdom. One of its most iconic landmarks is Mubende Hill, home to the legendary Nakayima Tree—a sacred fig tree believed to be over 350 years old. This site is linked to Nakayima, a priestess and spiritual figure from the Chwezi era, and remains an important place of worship and tradition to this day. Historically, Mubende was a key area in the Buganda Kingdom due to its high elevation, fertile land, and central location. It later served as a colonial administrative post under British rule. Mubende District was officially established in 1962, the same year Uganda gained independence. Over the years, it has evolved into a vibrant hub for agriculture, mining, and tourism, while still preserving its deep cultural roots. In 2018, part of Mubende was carved out to form the new Kassanda District.
